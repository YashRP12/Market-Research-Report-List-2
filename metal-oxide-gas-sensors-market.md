<p><p>Metal oxide gas sensors are classified into two main types: transition-metal oxides and non-transition-metal oxides. Transition-metal oxides, such as titanium and tungsten oxides, exhibit unique chemical properties that enable them to effectively detect gases like hydrogen, carbon monoxide, and nitrogen dioxide. On the other hand, non-transition-metal oxides, like tin and zinc oxides, are commonly used for sensing gases such as methane and ethanol. Both types of metal oxide gas sensors play a crucial role in various industries by providing reliable and accurate gas detection capabilities.</p></p>
