<p><p>Monolithic ceramics are categorized into two types: oxides and non-oxides. Oxides are ceramic materials composed of metal and oxygen atoms, such as alumina, zirconia, and silica. These materials possess excellent thermal and electrical insulating properties, making them suitable for use in various industries including electronics and aerospace. Non-oxides, on the other hand, consist of ceramics made up of elements like carbides, nitrides, and borides. These non-oxide ceramics exhibit superior mechanical strength, hardness, and thermal conductivity, making them ideal for applications requiring high wear resistance and thermal management.</p></p>
